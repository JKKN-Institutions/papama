
 However, there are several important business rules and operational controls that I would recommend explicitly including in Phase 1 rather than postponing to Phase 2.

Critical Items Missing or Need Clarification

1. Meal Entitlement Rules (Very Important)

The scope mentions meal redemption but does not clearly define:

Maximum meals per beneficiary per day.
Time windows for Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, and Snacks.
Whether a beneficiary can redeem at multiple vendors on the same day.
Cooling period between meal redemptions. This should be adjustable by admin in multi levels. 
Suggested Rule:

Breakfast: 6 AM – 10 AM
Lunch: 11 AM – 3 PM
Dinner: 6 PM – 10 PM
Maximum 3 meals per day. Initially it might be only one meal a day 


2. Token Sponsorship by Donors

A key pApAmA concept is:

Donate ₹50 = Sponsor 1 meal.
Donate ₹500 = Sponsor 10 meals.
Donate ₹5,000 = Sponsor 100 meals.
The donor should clearly see:

Number of meals sponsored.
Meals redeemed.
Meals pending.
This should be mandatory in Phase 1.



3. Beneficiary Co-Contribution

One of your core principles is dignity.

The system should allow:

Vendor collects ₹0–₹-10 or configurable amount.
Admin can set category-wise contribution.
No beneficiary should be denied food if unable to pay.
This business rule should be documented.



4. Vendor Capacity Management

Missing:

Daily meal capacity declaration.
Vendor availability status.
Temporary closure facility.
Meal stock exhausted notification.
Without this, donors may sponsor meals but vendors may not be able to serve them.



5. Geographic Service Coverage

The scope mentions geo-fencing but does not address:

City-wise rollout.
Vendor service zones.
Beneficiary search for nearby vendors.
The beneficiary app should display:

Nearby approved vendors.
Distance.
Operating hours.


6. Donor Impact Dashboard

Current scope mentions statistics but not detailed impact.

Should show:

Meals funded.
Meals served.
Beneficiary categories served.
City-wise impact.
Repeat donor statistics.
This is important for donor retention.



7. Corporate CSR Module

Since CSR funding can become a major source of donations:

Need:

CSR donor registration.
CSR reports.
Downloadable utilization certificates.
Impact reports.
Even a basic version should be in Phase 1.



8. Exception & Emergency Handling

Missing:

Disaster relief mode.
Temporary increase in meal limits.
Emergency token issuance.
For floods, cyclones, accidents, etc.



9. Vendor Rating & Audit System

Need:

Beneficiary feedback.
Surprise inspections.
Vendor quality score.
Suspension mechanism.
Food quality directly impacts pApAmA’s reputation.



10. Settlement Audit Controls

Current scope says photo and bill upload.

Also include:

Duplicate photo detection.
Duplicate bill detection.
Settlement hold facility.
Random audit queue.
This protects against misuse.



11. NGO / Institution Beneficiary Module

You had discussed:

Orphanages.
Old age homes.
Charity hospitals.
Need:

Bulk token allocation.
Bulk redemption tracking.
Institution-wise reporting.


12. Financial Controls

Very important for trust.

Need:

Donation ledger.
Vendor payable ledger.
Admin revenue ledger.
Settlement reconciliation reports.
Every rupee should be traceable.



13. Volunteer Management

The scope mentions volunteer-assisted registration but not volunteer management.

Need:

Volunteer onboarding.
Volunteer approval.
Activity tracking.
Area allocation.


14. Public Transparency Dashboard

A powerful pApAmA feature.

Display:

Total donations received.
Meals sponsored.
Meals served.
Active vendors.
Active beneficiaries.
Cities covered.
This builds credibility.



With these additions, the Phase 1 scope would be sufficiently robust to launch pApAmA as a controlled, transparent, and scalable humanitarian platform while remaining practical for the Coimbatore pilot.
